Music and Emotion Study

There are many trials in this study. On every trial, you will hear a short musical piece. Your task is to indicate the 'valence' and 'arousal' of each song while it is playing. This is done by moving your mouse over the graph.

  • Valence relates to the emotion conveyed by the music, and falls on a scale from positive to negative.

  • Arousal relates to the energy level conveyed by the music, and falls on a scale from high to low.

It is important to note that arousal and valence can change over the course of a song. So please make sure that you track arousal and valence as you perceive it at any given point in time.

Music and Emotion Study

For each question in the study:

  1. Click in the center of the graph to start the music
  2. Your mouse position will be tracked over the graph. So move your mouse to the area (high/low valence / arousal) that corresponds to your perceived emotion.
  3. You can move your mouse during the music, to match current perceived emotions.

If you perceive song as high in arousal (energy), you would move towards the top of this interface. Conversely, if you perceive the song as low in arousal (energy), you would move towards the bottom of the interface.

If you perceive the song as having a positively valenced emotion, you would move towards the right side of the interface. If you perceive the song as having a negatively valenced emotion, you would move towards the left side of the interface.

Please make sure to use the entire interface to accurately reflect of how you perceive the song.
